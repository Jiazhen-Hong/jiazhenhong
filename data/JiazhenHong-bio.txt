Jiazhen Hong is a final-year Ph.D. candidate in Computer Engineering at Rutgers University, advised by Professor Laleh Najafizadeh. His research focuses on machine learning, foundation models, and large language models for EEG and neural time-series signals, with applications in brain-computer interfaces and assistive communication. He develops LLM-driven P300 speller systems that improve real-time BCI communication for people with severe motor impairments, including ALS. His work has appeared in Nature Scientific Reports, IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering, IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering, NeurIPS, ICDM, EMBC, NER, and ISBI.

At EMOTIV, Jiazhen has worked on large-scale EEG foundation models, self-supervised representation learning, long-sequence EEG modeling, and distributed cloud training for neural signal data. His related work includes first-author publications at NeurIPS and IEEE ICDM workshops.

He holds an M.Sc. from Stevens Institute of Technology, B.Sc. double degrees from Jimei University and Chung Yuan Christian University, and previously conducted machine learning and biostatistics research at Harvard Medical School.
