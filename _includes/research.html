A Geometric Approach to k-means
Jiazhen Hong, Wei Qian, Yudong Chen, Yuqian Zhang
[IEEE TKDE 2025] IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering
We introduce a geometric framework for k-means that characterizes the structure of local optima in clustering. The approach reveals the geometric relationships between solutions and provides theoretical guarantees for cluster detection under diverse data distributions.

ChatBCI: A P300 Speller BCI with Context-Driven Word Prediction Leveraging Large Language Models
Jiazhen Hong, Weinan Wang, Laleh Najafizadeh
[Nature Portfolio 2026] Scientific Reports
We introduce ChatBCI, a P300 speller that integrates large language models to provide context-driven word prediction during EEG-based spelling. The system significantly reduces keystrokes and writing time (-53% and -62%) while increasing information transfer rate by 229% compared to conventional letter-by-letter BCI spellers.

ChatBCI-Assist: An Intent-Based P300 Speller with a Locally-Deployed LLM and Adaptive Stopping Strategy
Jiazhen Hong, Pradyumna Rao, Weinan Wang, Laleh Najafizadeh
[IEEE TBME Special Issue] IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering
We introduce ChatBCI-Assist, an intent-based P300 speller that integrates a locally-deployed large language model with an adaptive stopping strategy for real-time EEG-based communication. The system achieves record-level online performance, reaching 30.7 characters/min (105.2 bits/min ITR) and 147.1 bits/min semantic information transfer rate, enabling faster and more natural sentence composition in practical BCI communication.
SpellerSSL: Self-Supervised Learning with P300 Aggregation for Speller BCIs
Jiazhen Hong, Geoff Mackellar, Soheila Ghane
[NeurIPS 2025] Workshop on Data on the Brain and Mind
We propose SpellerSSL, a self-supervised learning framework that combines P300 aggregation with cross-domain EEG pretraining to learn generalizable representations for speller BCIs. The method achieves 94% character recognition with only 7 repetitions, reaches 21.86 bits/min information transfer rate, and reduces calibration data requirements by 60%.
Enhancing Typing Speed in LLM-based P300 Speller BCIs Using A New Data-Driven Dynamic Stopping Strategy
Jiazhen Hong, Laleh Najafizadeh
NER 2025 · IEEE EMBS 12th Annual International Conference on Neural Engineering
We introduce a novel data-driven dynamic stopping strategy for LLM-based P300 speller BCIs, which adaptively determines when to finalize character selection based on real-time EEG decoding. Achieves over 260% improvement in information transfer rate compared to traditional methods.
